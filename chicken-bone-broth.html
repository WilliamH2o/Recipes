Chicken Bone Broth (Pressure Cooker)

A rich, collagen-heavy broth made from chicken leg quarters and paws, pressure-cooked with apple cider vinegar to draw out minerals from the bones. Makes approximately 3–4 quarts.

Equipment

  • 6-quart Instant Pot or pressure cooker
  • Fine-mesh strainer or flour sack towel

Ingredients

  • 3–4 chicken leg quarters (broken at the joint)
  • 5–6 chicken paws (nails trimmed and outer yellow skin/spots removed)
  • 1 Tbsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground white pepper
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 10–12 cups water (enough to submerge bones; do not exceed the 2/3 max fill line)
  • 2–3 celery stalks, roughly chopped

Instructions

  1. Break the leg quarters at the joints so they fit in the pot.
  2. Add the turmeric, white pepper, bay leaves, celery, and apple cider vinegar.
  3. Pour in water until the bones are covered, staying below the Max Fill line.
  4. Let the mixture soak in the cold water for 1 hour to allow the acid to begin mineral extraction.
  5. Close the lid and set the valve to seal. Pressure cook on Low Pressure for 120 minutes.
  6. When the cooking time ends, allow the pressure to release naturally, about 20–30 minutes. Do not use quick release.
  7. Open the lid and check the bones; they should be soft and easily crushed.
  8. Use a slotted spoon to remove the large solids, then strain the liquid through a fine-mesh sieve or cloth into jars.
  9. Refrigerate overnight and scrape the solidified fat from the top in the morning.
